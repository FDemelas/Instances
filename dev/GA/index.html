A Generalized Assignment (GA) instance is defined by a set $I$ of items and a set $J$ of bins. Each bin $j$ is associated with a certain capacity $c_j$. For each item $i \in I$ and each bin $j \in J$, $p_{ij}$ is the profit of assigning item $i$ to bin $j$, and $w_{ij}$ is the weight of item $i$ inside bin $j$.

Considering a binary variable $x_{ij}$ for each item and each bin that is equal to one if and only if item $i$ is assigned to bin $j$, the GA problem can be formulated as:

\[\begin{align} & \max_{\bm{x}} \sum_{i \in I} \sum_{j \in J} p_{ij}x_{ij} \\ & \sum_{j \in J} x_{ij} \leq 1 && \forall i \in I \\ & \sum_{i \in I} w_{ij} x_{ij} \leq c_j && \forall j \in J \\ & x_{ij} \in \{0,1\} && \forall i \in I ,\; \forall j\in J . \end{align}\]
